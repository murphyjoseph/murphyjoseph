## Pizza Recipes

### 1. Pan Pizza (3-Day Dough)

**Source:** @itsdoughguy

**Instructions:**

1. Oil up cast iron pan generously
2. Use 3-day cold fermented dough (recipe pinned on creator's page)
3. Press dough into oiled pan
4. Top with sauce, cheese, and toppings
5. Bake until crust is golden and cheese is melted
6. Let rest on cooling rack before serving

### 2. NY Style Pizza Sauce

**Source:** @itsdoughguy

**Ingredients:**

- 1 can (28 oz) San Marzano tomatoes
- 2 tsp sea salt
- Fresh basil
- Garlic
- Oregano
- Olive oil

**Instructions:**

Blend all ingredients until smooth. No cooking required - use raw on pizza.

### 3. South Shore Bar Pie Dough

**Source:** @seans_pizza
**Yield:** 4 × 10" pizzas
**Dough Ball Weight:** 185g
**Total Dough:** 740g

**Ingredients (Baker's Percentages):**

- Bread flour: 100%
- Water: 55-58%
- Salt: 2%
- Yeast: 0.5% (instant dry yeast)
- Oil: 2%

**Method:**

1. Mix dry ingredients
2. Add water and oil
3. Knead until smooth
4. Cold ferment 24-48 hours
5. Ball and proof at room temp before shaping

### 4. Cast Iron Pan Pizza

**Source:** @davepizzaoven
**Equipment:** Cast iron skillet

**Instructions:**

1. Generously oil cast iron skillet
2. Press room-temperature dough into pan
3. Let rise 30 minutes in pan
4. Top with sauce and cheese
5. Bake at 500°F until bubbly
6. Finish under broiler for crispy top

### 5. Sicilian Pizza

**Source:** @davepizzaoven
**Pan:** 12x12" square pan
**Dough Ball:** 550g

**Ingredients:**

- 225g Bread Flour
- 75g All Purpose Flour
- 225g Water (75% hydration)
- 6g Salt (2%)
- 6g Olive Oil (2%)
- 0.6g Instant Dry Yeast (0.2%)

**Method:**

1. Mix flour, salt, and yeast
2. Add water and oil, mix until shaggy
3. Rest 30 min, fold, rest again
4. Cold ferment 48-72 hours
5. Oil pan generously
6. Stretch dough into pan
7. Let proof 2-4 hours at room temp
8. Top and bake at 500°F

### 6. Detroit Style Pizza

**Source:** @davepizzaoven
**Pan:** 8x10" Detroit-style pan

**Dough Ingredients:**

- 160g bread flour
- 120g room temp water (75%)
- 3.2g salt (2%)
- 5g oil
- 0.3g instant dry yeast

**Instructions:**

1. Mix all ingredients
2. Fold and rest
3. Cold ferment 48-72 hours
4. Oil Detroit-style pan generously
5. Press dough into corners
6. Top with Wisconsin brick cheese to edges (creates frico)
7. Add toppings, then sauce in racing stripes
8. Bake at 500°F until cheese is caramelized on edges

### 7. Tavern Style (Chicago Thin Crust)

**Source:** @davepizzaoven / @thebkydpalate
**Characteristics:** Cracker-thin, cut into squares (party cut)

**Method:**

1. Use low-hydration dough (50-55%)
2. Roll very thin with rolling pin
3. Stretch to edges of screen or pan
4. Top with sauce, cheese edge-to-edge
5. Bake at highest oven temp until crispy
6. Cut into squares (tavern/party cut)

### 8. NY-ish Style Dough (48-72 Hour)

**Source:** @davepizzaoven
**Recommended fermentation:** 48-72 hours total

**Ingredients:**

- Bread flour
- Water (63-65% hydration)
- Salt (2%)
- Olive oil (2%)
- Instant dry yeast (0.2%)

**Instructions:**

1. Mix ingredients
2. Knead until smooth windowpane
3. Bulk ferment 2 hours
4. Ball and cold ferment 48-72 hours
5. Remove from fridge 3-4 hours before baking
6. Stretch by hand (don't use rolling pin)
7. Bake on preheated steel/stone at highest oven temp

### 9. Neapolitan Pizza Dough

**Source:** @betterpizzabureau
**Style:** Simple summer recipe

**Ingredients:**

- Tipo 00 flour
- Water (60-65% hydration)
- Salt (2.5-3%)
- Fresh yeast or instant dry yeast

**Method:**

1. Dissolve salt in water
2. Add flour, mix until combined
3. Knead 15-20 minutes until smooth
4. Bulk ferment 6-8 hours at room temp
5. Ball (250-280g per ball)
6. Proof 4-6 hours
7. Shape by hand
8. Bake at highest possible heat (ideally 800°F+)

### 10. 24-Hour Dough (Simple Version)

**Source:** @davepizzaoven
**Notes:** The go-to recipe for beginners

**Instructions:**

1. Mix flour, water, salt, oil, yeast
2. Knead until smooth
3. Let rise 30 minutes at room temp
4. Press flat and refrigerate
5. Cold ferment 24 hours
6. Remove 2-3 hours before baking
7. Shape and bake

### 11. Detroit Style Pizza Dough (Alternative)

**Source:** @cookingwithcarby

**Ingredients:**

- 625g '00' flour
- Water (70% hydration)
- Salt
- Yeast
- Olive oil

**Method:**

Use focaccia-style technique - oil the pan generously, let dough proof in pan, top with cheese to edges

### 12. New Haven Style (Apizza)

**Source:** @theloveofpizza

**Key characteristics:**

- Thin, charred crust
- Irregular shape
- Minimal toppings
- Baked on steel in home oven

**Method:**

1. Use high-hydration dough (65-68%)
2. Cold ferment 48+ hours
3. Stretch thin
4. Sauce sparingly
5. Bake on preheated steel at highest temp
6. Use broiler to achieve char

### 13. Pizza Bites (Homemade)

**Source:** @itsdoughguy
**Style:** Quick appetizer version

**Instructions:**

1. Roll out pizza dough
2. Cut into small rounds
3. Fill with cheese and mini pepperoni
4. Fold and seal edges
5. Brush with garlic butter
6. Bake until golden
7. Serve with marinara for dipping

### 14. Tri-Tip & Chimichurri Pizza

**Source:** @itsdoughguy

**Toppings:**

### 15. Grandma Style Pizza

## Equipment

### Pizza Steel vs. Pizza Stone

**Source:** @itsdoughguy

- **Pizza stones** work great if your oven hits 700-800°F
- **For home ovens (500-550°F):** Use a pizza steel instead
- Steel conducts heat better and gives restaurant-quality results at lower temps
- Preheat steel for 45-60 minutes at highest oven temp

### Choosing a Pizza Peel

**Source:** @scottspizzatours

- **Wooden peel:** Best for launching raw pizza (flour/semolina sticks less)
- **Metal peel:** Best for turning and retrieving cooked pizza (thinner, easier to slide under)
- **Pro tip:** Get both if you're serious about pizza making

## Dough Tips

### Why Ferment Pizza Dough 24-72 Hours?

**Source:** @payalthakurani

**Benefits of long fermentation:**

- Strengthens gluten → dough is easier to stretch
- Develops complex flavors → better taste
- Improves digestibility → easier on stomach
- Better texture → more airy, crispy crust

**Timing:**

- **Minimum:** 24 hours in fridge
- **Sweet spot:** 48-72 hours

### Salt Ratio Precision

**Source:** @eatbasilandbloom

- **Standard:** 2% salt (0.02 × flour weight)
- **Pro tip:** Some pizzaiolos use 0.96% instead of 1% for subtle difference
- After 10,000+ pizzas, small adjustments matter!

### Dough Prep Process

**Source:** @itsdoughguy

- Take dough out of fridge 3-4 hours before baking
- Let it come to room temperature
- This makes stretching much easier
- Cold dough = tears and holes

### Stretching Techniques

**Source:** @davepizzaoven

**For defined crust with thin, sturdy center:**

- Press from center outward, leaving edge untouched
- Use gravity - let dough hang and rotate
- Use backs of hands/knuckles, not fingers
- Don't use a rolling pin for NY style (kills air bubbles)
- Work quickly to prevent sticking

## Baking Tips

### Getting Pizzeria Char at Home

**Source:** @scottspizzatours

**The secret:** Use your BROILER!

1. Bake pizza on steel/stone at 500°F+ until almost done
2. Turn on broiler for last 1-2 minutes
3. Watch carefully - it chars fast!
4. Position pizza 4-6 inches from broiler element

### Avoiding "Swampy Top"

**Source:** @scottspizzatours

**The problem:** Fresh mozzarella releases too much moisture

**Solutions:**

- Slice fresh mozz and pat dry with paper towels
- Let sliced mozz sit out 30 min to dry surface
- Use less fresh mozz, supplement with low-moisture mozz
- Add fresh mozz halfway through bake

### Avoiding Greasy, Burnt Cheese

**Source:** @scottspizzatours

**Problem:** Crust isn't done but cheese is already burning

**Solutions:**

- Preheat steel/stone longer (45-60 min minimum)
- Use less cheese
- Start with higher heat, finish lower
- Try part-skim mozzarella (less fat = less grease)

## Pizza Styles Guide

### Know Your Thin Crusts

**Source:** @triggtube

**Types of thin crust pizza:**

- **New York:** Foldable, slight chew
- **New Haven (Apizza):** Charred, very thin
- **Bar Pie:** Cracker-thin, edge-to-edge toppings
- **Tavern (Chicago thin):** Cracker crust, party cut
- **Roman (al taglio):** Crispy, rectangular, sold by weight

**Note:** Detroit Style = THICK crust (not thin!)

### St. Louis Style

**Source:** @davepizzaoven

- Uses **Provel cheese** (blend of cheddar, swiss, provolone)
- Provel turns orange when melted
- Cracker-thin crust
- Cut into squares (party cut)

## Ingredient Tips

### Pizza Sauce - Keep It Simple!

**Source:** @scottspizzatours

**The truth:** We overthink pizza sauce!

**Simple approach:**

1. Use quality canned tomatoes (crushed or whole)
2. Add salt
3. That's it! Don't cook it.

**Tomato options:**

- **San Marzano DOP** (best, but expensive)
- **6-in-1 brand** (pizzeria favorite)
- **Bianco DiNapoli** (high quality)
- Any quality crushed tomato works!

### Cheese for Detroit Style

**Source:** Various

- **Authentic:** Wisconsin brick cheese
- **Alternative:** 50/50 brick cheese + low-moisture mozzarella
- Must go to edges to create crispy frico cheese edge

### Hot Honey on Pizza

**Source:** @davepizzaoven

- Perfect finishing drizzle for bar pies
- Adds sweet heat that complements pepperoni
- Add AFTER baking, not before

## General Dos & Don'ts

### ✅ DO:

- Preheat your steel/stone for at least 45-60 minutes
- Use room temperature dough (take out 2-4 hours early)
- Ferment dough at least 24 hours for best flavor
- Use a scale for accuracy (baking is science!)
- Use semolina or flour on peel to prevent sticking
- Work quickly once dough is stretched
- Let pizza rest 1-2 minutes before cutting

### ❌ DON'T:

- Use a rolling pin for NY-style (kills air bubbles)
- Overload with toppings (soggy pizza)
- Open oven door constantly (loses heat)
- Skip the cold ferment (loses flavor/texture)
- Use cold dough straight from fridge
- Put fresh mozzarella on without drying it first
- Cut immediately (let cheese set up)
